Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What kind of power outlets are used on Taniti?

Taniti uses 120-volt power outlets, the same as those used in the United States.

Q: What are the alcohol laws on Taniti?

A: Alcohol cannot be served or sold between midnight and 9:00 a.m. The legal drinking age is 18, though it is not strictly enforced.

Q: Is English widely spoken on the island?

A: Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English, especially in urban and tourist areas. However, in rural regions—particularly among older residents—English is rarely spoken.

Q: What medical facilities are available on Taniti?

A: There is one hospital and several clinics on the island. The hospital has many multilingual staff members to assist international visitors.

Q: Is Taniti safe for tourists?

A: Violent crime is very rare. However, as tourism grows, incidents of pickpocketing and petty theft have become more common. Visitors should take normal safety precautions.

Q: Are there any important holidays I should be aware of?

A: Taniti celebrates many national holidays. On these days, tourist attractions and restaurants may be closed, so plan your activities and dining accordingly.

Q: What currency is used on Taniti?

A: The U.S. dollar is the official currency, but many businesses also accept euros and yen. Currency exchange is available at several banks, and most major credit cards are widely accepted.
